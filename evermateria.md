#### STREAM 1: CEMENT / BINDER (GEOPOLYMER + FERROCK)

**Problem:** Common building materials, such as concrete and steel, tend to be energy- and carbon-intensive to produce.

**Solution:** Replace Portland Cement entirely with two waste-derived binders:

**A. GEOPOLYMER CEMENT**
- **Feedstock:** Fly ash (coal plant waste) + Ground Granulated Blast Furnace Slag (GGBS, steel mill waste) + alkaline activator (NaOH from brine electrolysis via renewable energy)
- **Process:** Ambient-temperature curing (no kiln) — 80-90% less CO₂ than OPC
- **Performance:** Equal or superior compressive strength to OPC, excellent chemical resistance
- **Cost:** Feedstocks are industrial wastes — negative or zero cost inputs
- **Circularity:** End-of-life geopolymer concrete → crushed → re-activated → new geopolymer

**B. FERROCK**
- Ferrock has gained attention for its unique carbon-negative properties and strength-enhancing characteristics. Ferrock is a novel material developed by utilizing waste steel dust and other silica-rich materials, such as fly ash, limestone powder, and metakaolin.
- **Key advantage:** Carbon-NEGATIVE — absorbs CO₂ during curing
- A 10% Ferrock addition offers an optimal balance of mechanical performance, durability, and environmental benefits, supporting its potential as a low-carbon, cost-effective material.
- **Profit:** Carbon credits from sequestration during production

**Supplementary Cementitious Materials (SCMs):**
- Various industrial waste materials—such as fly ash, coal bottom ash, palm oil fuel ash, sugarcane bagasse ash, and ground granulated blast furnace slag—as supplementary cementitious materials. The evaluation focuses on their properties, environmental benefits, and potential to enhance concrete sustainability. The findings reveal that these industrial wastes can significantly reduce the carbon footprint.
- SDA and BLA, at controlled replacement levels, can serve as effective supplementary cementitious materials in sandcrete brick production. Their use supports sustainable construction practices by reducing reliance on Portland cement.

**Profit Streams from Cement:**
| Product | Source | Market |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Carbon credits | CO₂ absorbed by Ferrock | Carbon markets |
| Surplus geopolymer | Excess production | Commercial construction |
| Pre-cast elements | High-value finishing | Architecture market |
| SCM blends | Optimized ash mixes | Cement industry |
| Waste processing fees | Taking industrial waste | Industrial clients |

#### STREAM 2: STEEL (100% CIRCULAR RECYCLED)

**Problem:** Virgin steel from ore is enormously energy-intensive.

**Solution:** 100% recycled steel via Electric Arc Furnace (EAF)

- EoL reinforcement steel is 1–11% reused and 70–95% recycled. — The infrastructure for steel recycling already exists at industrial scale.

**Feedstock Sources (All Free or Negative-Cost):**
- Demolition steel (paid to collect)
- End-of-life vehicles (junkyards pay to remove)
- Industrial scrap (paid to remove)
- Obsolete infrastructure (bridge/rail decommission)
- Appliance recycling (paid to collect)

**Process:**
1. **Collection & Sorting:** Magnetic separation + manual grading
2. **Shredding:** Mechanical size reduction
3. **EAF Melting:** 100% renewable electricity (solar/wind) → ~75% less energy than BOF
4. **Casting & Rolling:** Standard metallurgical processes
5. **Quality Grading:** Structural, reinforcement, architectural grades

**Profit Streams from Steel:**
| Product | Source | Market |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Surplus structural steel | Excess production | Commercial/industrial |
| Specialty alloys | Premium metallurgy | Automotive/aerospace |
| Steel slag | EAF byproduct | Cement SCM / road base |
| Collection fees | Taking scrap | Industries/demolition |
| Iron oxide/rust | Corrosion recovery | Pigments/Ferrock feedstock |
| Recycling certificates | Verified circularity | ESG compliance markets |

#### STREAM 3: BRICK (ECO-BRICK — WASTE-DERIVED)

**Solution:** Compressed Earth Blocks (CEB) + Waste-Plastic Eco-Bricks + Geopolymer Bricks

**A. Compressed Earth Blocks (CEB)**
- **Feedstock:** Local subsoil + small % geopolymer stabilizer
- **Process:** Hydraulic compression, no firing (zero energy curing)
- **Performance:** Excellent thermal mass, meets structural codes
- **Circularity:** End-of-life → crush → re-compress

**B. Waste-Plastic Eco-Bricks**
- Plastic waste used in making the bricks includes PVC, which has few recycling solutions, concrete waste, glass waste, and locally sourced cement substitutes. Alternatives, such as pulverized fly ash from coal power plants or granulated blast slag from steel mills, may be used.
- Any waste generated, including the bricks, can be recycled back into bricks. So, the goal is zero waste from start to finish.

**C. Geopolymer Bricks**
- Fly ash + slag + alkaline activator, pressed and cured
- No kiln firing, ambient temperature

**Profit Streams from Brick:**

| Plastic waste processing | Taking plastic waste | Municipalities |
| Surplus bricks | Excess production | Commercial market |

#### STREAM 4: ASPHALT / TAR (RUBBERIZED + BIO-BASED)

**Solution:** Bio-asphalt + Waste-Tire Rubberized Asphalt

**A. Bio-Asphalt**
- **Feedstock:** Lignin (paper mill waste), pyrolysis bio-oil (agricultural waste), waste cooking oil
- **Process:** Thermal processing of bio-feedstocks → bio-binder
- **Performance:** Equivalent to petroleum asphalt
- **Circularity:** Bio-based = biodegradable; can be re-processed

**B. Rubberized Asphalt**
- **Feedstock:** Waste tires (negative cost — tire disposal fees)
- **Process:** Crumb rubber → blend with bio-binder + recycled aggregate
- **Performance:** Superior crack resistance, noise reduction, longer life

**C. Recycled Asphalt Pavement (RAP)**
- Old asphalt roads → milled → reprocessed → new asphalt
- 100% re-use of existing road material

**Profit Streams from Asphalt:**

5. Quality testing: particle size distribution, LA abrasion, density
