### NEW: Biotin (B7)

Not confirmed present in any current stack layer. Genuine addition.

Biotin is the cofactor for **5 carboxylase enzymes** — none of which are redundant:

```
Acetyl-CoA carboxylase     → fatty acid synthesis initiation
Pyruvate carboxylase       → gluconeogenesis (glucose homeostasis)
Propionyl-CoA carboxylase  → odd-chain fatty acid + amino acid catabolism
β-Methylcrotonyl-CoA       → leucine catabolism ← CRITICAL
3-Methylcrotonyl-CoA       → branched-chain amino acid metabolism
```

**Critical stack-specific synergy:** HMB is synthesised endogenously from leucine via the β-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase reaction — which is **biotin-dependent**. Biotin deficiency directly impairs your body's endogenous HMB production from the whey leucine in Ensure Gold. Supplemental HMB already addresses this, but adequate biotin maximises the conversion.

Additionally: histone biotinylation (Hallmark 3 — epigenetic alterations) is a direct biotin-dependent mechanism. Biotin modifies histones H2A, H3, and H4, repressing repeat elements and maintaining chromatin stability.

Sources in Cape Town context: avocado, sweet potato, mushrooms, egg yolk, sunflower seeds, lentils, liver.

### OTHER HIGH-PRIORITY ADDITIONS

**1. NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine) — MAJOR GAP**

Cysteine is the rate-limiting substrate for glutathione (GSH) synthesis. The current stack has ALA (GSH regenerator) but no **cysteine replenishment**. ALA recycles existing GSH but cannot replace lost cysteine pool. NAC directly provides the missing third of the GSH triad:

```
Glutamate (from protein/Ensure) + Cysteine [NAC] + Glycine → Glutathione
```

Synergy: ALA regenerates oxidised GSH → NAC replenishes the cysteine pool → together they make glutathione synthesis effectively continuous. Neither achieves this without the other. NAC also independently scavenges ROS and has mucolytic/respiratory protective effects, and supports the SOD cascade by providing GSH substrate for GPx to use.

**2. Glycine — STRUCTURAL + GLUTATHIONE + SLEEP GAP**

Glycine is the most abundant amino acid in collagen (33%), yet it is the most commonly limiting substrate because dietary intake rarely covers synthesis demand. The collagen layer (Layer 4) is functionally incomplete without glycine supplementation even when all enzymatic cofactors are present.

Additional roles:
- Third GSH substrate (alongside cysteine from NAC above)
- NMDA receptor glycine co-agonist site modulation — sleep quality, memory consolidation
- Detoxification conjugation in liver (hippuric acid pathway)
- Serine synthesis (one-carbon folate metabolism)

Synergy with NAC: NAC provides cysteine (GSH component 2); glycine provides component 3; glutamate already present from protein digestion. The glutathione triad is only fully completed when NAC + Glycine are both present.

**3. Apigenin — NAD⁺ PRESERVATION (Critical)**

The triple NAD⁺ route (NMNH + NADH + B3) is currently the stack's most potent longevity mechanism — but all three face an underappreciated drain: **CD38**, a glycohydrolase that consumes NAD⁺ as a substrate and whose expression increases exponentially with age. CD38 is the primary reason NAD⁺ precursor supplementation raises intracellular NAD⁺ less than expected.

Apigenin is the most bioavailable natural CD38 inhibitor identified to date:

```
NMNH/NADH/B3 → NAD⁺ ↑↑
CD38 [uninhibited] → consumes NAD⁺
Net: moderate NAD⁺ elevation

NMNH/NADH/B3 → NAD⁺ ↑↑
Apigenin → CD38 inhibited → NAD⁺ preserved
Net: substantially higher NAD⁺ elevation
```

Apigenin also activates SIRT1 independently. Sources in Cape Town: **chamomile** (Matricaria chamomilla — grows in Cape gardens), parsley, celery — all cultivable in Cape Town's climate.

**6. TMG (Trimethylglycine/Betaine) — Methylation**

TMG is a methyl donor that feeds the SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) cycle — the universal methylation substrate. B12 + Folate (Ensure Gold) catalyse the methylation cycle, but TMG provides the methyl groups themselves. Without adequate methyl donors, homocysteine accumulates — a driver of genomic instability (Hallmark 1) and vascular damage. Additionally, demethylated TMG yields glycine (synergising with the glycine addition above).
