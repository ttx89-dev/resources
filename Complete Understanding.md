## FOUNDATION LAYER: Universal Principles

### 1. MATHEMATICS (The Language)
**Core Concepts:**
- Numbers: Counting, operations, ratios, proportions
- Algebra: Variables, equations, functions, graphs
- Geometry: Shapes, areas, volumes, angles, trigonometry
- Calculus: Rates of change, accumulation, optimization
- Statistics: Probability, distributions, inference

**Why It Matters:** Math describes patterns. Everything that works follows mathematical rules.

### 2. PHYSICS (The Rules)

#### 2.1 Mechanics (Motion & Forces)
**Fundamentals:**
- Newton's Laws: Force = M × Acceleration, Action-Reaction, Inertia
- Energy: Kinetic (motion), Potential (position), Conservation
- Momentum: M × Velocity, conserved in collisions
- Rotation: Torque, angular momentum, gyroscopes
- Friction: Resistance between surfaces, heat generation

**Applications:** Vehicles, machines, structures, sports, space travel

#### 2.2 Thermodynamics (Heat & Energy)
**Laws:**
1. Energy is conserved (cannot create/destroy)
2. Entropy increases (heat flows hot→cold, disorder increases)
3. Absolute zero unreachable (-273.15°C)

**Key Concepts:**
- Temperature: Average kinetic energy of molecules
- Heat transfer: Conduction (contact), Convection (fluid flow), Radiation (waves)
- Engines: Convert heat → work via temperature difference
- Refrigeration: Move heat against natural flow (requires work)

**Applications:** Engines, HVAC, cooking, power plants, refrigerators

#### 2.3 Waves & Oscillations
**Principles:**
- Wave properties: Frequency, wavelength, amplitude, speed
- Types: Transverse (perpendicular) vs Longitudinal (parallel)
- Resonance: Amplification at natural frequency
- Interference: Waves add/cancel when overlapping

**Applications:** Sound, music, earthquakes, radio, microwaves

#### 2.4 Electricity & Magnetism
**Fundamentals:**
- Charge: Positive/negative, attraction/repulsion
- Current: Flow of charge (Amperes)
- Voltage: Electrical pressure/potential difference (Volts)
- Resistance: Opposition to flow (Ohms)
- Ohm's Law: V = I × R

**Magnetism:**
- Moving charges create magnetic fields
- Changing magnetic fields create electric fields
- Motors: Electricity → Motion
- Generators: Motion → Electricity

**Applications:** All electronics, motors, power grid, computers, phones

#### 2.5 Light & Optics
**Nature:**
- Dual: Wave and particle (photon)
- Speed: 300,000 km/s in vacuum
- Spectrum: Radio → Microwave → Infrared → Visible → UV → X-ray → Gamma

**Behavior:**
- Reflection: Angle in = Angle out (mirrors)
- Refraction: Bending when changing medium (lenses)
- Diffraction: Spreading around obstacles
- Interference: Creating patterns

**Applications:** Vision, cameras, fiber optics, lasers, solar panels

#### 2.6 Modern Physics
**Quantum Mechanics:**
- Energy comes in discrete packets (quanta)
- Uncertainty: Cannot know position and momentum precisely
- Wave-particle duality: Everything has wave properties
- Atomic structure: Electrons in energy levels

**Relativity:**
- Special: Time/space relative to observer, E=mc²
- General: Gravity is curved spacetime

**Applications:** Semiconductors, lasers, GPS, nuclear power, medical imaging

### 3. CHEMISTRY (Matter & Transformations)

#### 3.1 Atomic Structure
- Nucleus: Protons (positive), Neutrons (neutral)
- Electrons: Negative, orbit nucleus in shells
- Elements: Defined by proton number (Periodic Table)
- Isotopes: Same element, different neutron count

#### 3.2 Bonding
- Ionic: Transfer electrons (metal + nonmetal) → salts
- Covalent: Share electrons (nonmetals) → molecules
- Metallic: Electron sea → conductivity, malleability
- Hydrogen: Weak attraction → water properties

#### 3.3 Reactions
- Combustion: Fuel + Oxygen → CO₂ + H₂O + Energy
- Oxidation-Reduction: Electron transfer (rust, batteries)
- Acid-Base: Proton transfer (pH scale)
- Catalysts: Speed reactions without being consumed

#### 3.4 States of Matter
- Solid: Fixed shape, fixed volume (strong bonds)
- Liquid: Fixed volume, flows (moderate bonds)
- Gas: Fills container (weak bonds)
- Plasma: Ionized gas (very high energy)
- Phase changes: Melting, boiling, condensation, freezing

**Applications:** Batteries, fuel, plastics, drugs, cleaning, cooking, materials

### 4. MATERIALS SCIENCE

#### 4.1 Metals
- Properties: Conductive, strong, ductile, malleable
- Types: Steel (iron+carbon), Aluminum (light), Copper (conductive), Titanium (strong+light)
- Processing: Casting, forging, machining, welding, heat treatment
- Applications: Structure, wiring, engines, tools

#### 4.2 Ceramics & Glass
- Properties: Hard, brittle, heat-resistant, insulating
- Types: Porcelain, concrete, brick, glass, semiconductors
- Applications: Buildings, cookware, electronics, optics

#### 4.3 Polymers (Plastics)
- Structure: Long chain molecules
- Types: Thermoplastic (remeltable), Thermoset (permanent)
- Properties: Light, moldable, insulating, variable strength
- Applications: Packaging, clothing, pipes, composites

#### 4.4 Composites
- Combination of materials for superior properties
- Examples: Fiberglass, carbon fiber, concrete (cement+aggregate)
- Applications: Aerospace, sports equipment, construction

## SYSTEM LAYER: How Things Work

### 5. MECHANICAL SYSTEMS

#### 5.1 Simple Machines (Force Multipliers)
1. **Lever:** Fulcrum + beam, multiply force or distance
2. **Wheel & Axle:** Continuous lever (steering, gears)
3. **Pulley:** Change direction, multiply force
4. **Inclined Plane:** Trade distance for force (ramps)
5. **Wedge:** Convert forward force to sideways (knife, axe)
6. **Screw:** Inclined plane wrapped around cylinder

#### 5.2 Transmissions & Gears
- Gears: Transfer rotation, change speed/torque ratio
- Belt/Chain: Transfer power between separated shafts
- Clutch: Engage/disengage power transfer
- Differential: Allow wheels to rotate at different speeds

**Applications:** Vehicles, bicycles, machines, clocks

#### 5.3 Engines
**Internal Combustion:**
1. Intake: Fuel+air enter cylinder
2. Compression: Piston compresses mixture
3. Ignition: Spark ignites, expansion pushes piston
4. Exhaust: Waste gases expelled
- Types: Gasoline (spark), Diesel (compression ignition)

**External Combustion:**
- Steam: Heat water → steam → drive piston/turbine
- Stirling: Heat/cool gas to drive piston

**Applications:** Cars, generators, ships, planes

#### 5.4 Hydraulics & Pneumatics
- Principle: Pressure in fluid transmits force
- Pascal's Law: Pressure = Force/Area
- Hydraulic: Incompressible liquid (oil) → precise, powerful
- Pneumatic: Compressed air → fast, clean, safe

**Applications:** Brakes, construction equipment, doors, tools

---

### 6. ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS

#### 6.1 Power Generation
**Methods:**
- Mechanical rotation → electromagnetic induction
- Sources: Water (hydro), Steam (coal/gas/nuclear), Wind, Solar (photovoltaic)
- AC vs DC: Alternating (grid) vs Direct (batteries)

#### 6.2 Power Distribution
- Transformer: Change voltage (step up for transmission, down for use)
- Three-phase: Efficient power delivery (industrial)
- Circuit protection: Fuses, breakers prevent overload/fire

#### 6.3 Circuits & Components
**Basic Components:**
- Resistor: Limit current, drop voltage
- Capacitor: Store charge temporarily, filter signals
- Inductor: Store energy in magnetic field, resist AC
- Diode: Allow current one direction only
- Transistor: Amplify or switch current (fundamental to computers)

**Circuit Types:**
- Series: Components in line (same current)
- Parallel: Components side-by-side (same voltage)

#### 6.4 Motors
- DC Motor: Permanent magnets + rotating coil
- AC Motor: Rotating magnetic field drives rotor
- Stepper: Precise position control via pulses
- Servo: Feedback-controlled position

**Applications:** Fans, pumps, vehicles, robots, hard drives

---

### 7. ELECTRONIC SYSTEMS

#### 7.1 Semiconductors
- Silicon: 4 valence electrons, can be doped
- N-type: Extra electrons (negative carriers)
- P-type: Electron holes (positive carriers)
- P-N junction: Basis of diodes, transistors, solar cells

#### 7.2 Digital Logic
**Binary:** All information as 1s and 0s
- AND: Output 1 only if all inputs 1
- OR: Output 1 if any input 1
- NOT: Invert input
- Combinations build: Memory, processors, computers

#### 7.3 Processors & Memory
**CPU (Central Processing Unit):**
- Fetch instruction from memory
- Decode what operation to perform
- Execute operation
- Store result
- Repeat billions of times per second

**Memory:**
- RAM: Fast, temporary (volatile)
- ROM: Permanent instructions
- Storage: Hard drives (magnetic), SSD (flash memory)

#### 7.4 Communication
**Analog:**
- Continuous signal varies with information
- AM (amplitude modulation), FM (frequency modulation)

**Digital:**
- Information as discrete bits
- Error correction, encryption possible
- Internet: Packet switching, TCP/IP protocols

**Wireless:**
- Radio waves carry information
- WiFi, Bluetooth, Cellular, Satellite

### 8. FLUID SYSTEMS

#### 8.1 Fluid Statics
- Pressure increases with depth: P = ρgh
- Buoyancy: Upward force = weight of displaced fluid
- Applications: Dams, ships, submarines, balloons

#### 8.2 Fluid Dynamics
- Bernoulli: Fast flow = low pressure
- Viscosity: Resistance to flow
- Turbulence vs Laminar flow
- Applications: Airplane wings, pipes, pumps, ventilation

#### 8.3 Pumps & Compressors
- Positive displacement: Move fixed volume (piston, gear)
- Centrifugal: Spin fluid outward
- Applications: Water supply, HVAC, fuel delivery

---

### 9. THERMAL SYSTEMS

#### 9.1 Heating Systems
**Furnace/Boiler:**
- Burn fuel → heat air/water
- Distribution: Ducts (forced air), Pipes (hot water/steam)

**Heat Pump:**
- Move heat using refrigeration cycle
- Heating mode: Extract heat from outside
- Reversible for cooling

**Electric:**
- Resistance heating (inefficient but simple)
- Radiant (infrared panels)

#### 9.2 Cooling Systems
**Refrigeration Cycle:**
1. Compressor: Pressurize refrigerant (heats up)
2. Condenser: Release heat to outside (liquid)
3. Expansion valve: Drop pressure (cools down)
4. Evaporator: Absorb heat from inside (gas)
5. Return to compressor

**Applications:** Refrigerators, freezers, air conditioners, heat pumps

#### 9.3 Insulation
- Reduce heat transfer via conduction, convection, radiation
- Materials: Fiberglass, foam, air gaps, reflective barriers
- R-value: Thermal resistance (higher = better)

### 10. CHEMICAL SYSTEMS

#### 10.1 Energy Storage
**Batteries:**
- Chemical → Electrical via redox reactions
- Primary: Single-use (alkaline)
- Secondary: Rechargeable (lithium-ion, lead-acid)

**Fuel Cells:**
- Hydrogen + Oxygen → Water + Electricity
- High efficiency, clean (water only)

#### 10.2 Combustion
- Fuel + Oxygen → Products + Heat
- Requirements: Fuel, oxygen, ignition temperature
- Control: Ratio, temperature, pressure
- Applications: Heating, cooking, engines, power generation

#### 10.3 Water Treatment
**Purification:**
- Filtration: Remove particles
- Disinfection: Kill microorganisms (chlorine, UV, ozone)
- Softening: Remove minerals (ion exchange)

**Wastewater:**
- Primary: Settling removes solids
- Secondary: Bacteria digest organics
- Tertiary: Advanced treatment (nutrients, chemicals)

## KEY PRINCIPLES (Remember Always)

### 1. Conservation Laws
- Energy cannot be created or destroyed (only transformed)
- Momentum is conserved in isolated systems
- Charge is conserved
- Mass-energy is conserved

### 2. Efficiency
- All real processes lose energy to heat
- No process is 100% efficient
- Carnot limit for heat engines: η = 1 - (Tcold/Thot)

### 3. Feedback & Control
- Sensors measure state
- Comparator finds error (desired - actual)
- Actuator corrects
- Thermostat example: Measure temp → Compare to setpoint → Turn heat on/off

### 4. Scaling
- Linear dimensions × N → Area × N² → Volume × N³
- Strength scales with area (N²)
- Weight scales with volume (N³)
- Explains why large animals have thicker legs

### 5. Optimization Trade-offs
- Cost vs Performance
- Speed vs Efficiency
- Strength vs Weight
- Complexity vs Reliability

## PRACTICAL APPLICATION GUIDE

### Troubleshooting Method
1. **Define problem:** What specifically isn't working?
2. **Understand normal:** How should it work?
3. **Isolate:** Where in the system is the failure?
4. **Test:** Check inputs, outputs, intermediate stages
5. **Fix:** Replace failed component or adjust settings
6. **Verify:** Confirm normal operation restored

### Design Process
1. **Requirements:** What must it do?
2. **Constraints:** Limits on size, cost, materials, time
3. **Concepts:** Multiple solution approaches
4. **Analysis:** Calculate performance, identify best
5. **Prototype:** Build and test
6. **Iterate:** Refine based on testing
7. **Implement:** Final production

### Learning Any New Technology
1. **Purpose:** What problem does it solve?
2. **Inputs/Outputs:** What goes in, what comes out?
3. **Energy flow:** How is energy/signal transformed?
4. **Key components:** What are the main parts?
5. **Operation sequence:** Step-by-step process
6. **Failure modes:** What can go wrong and why?
7. **Hands-on:** Touch, observe, experiment

## FOUNDATIONAL EQUATIONS (Most Important)

**Mechanics:**
- F = ma (Force = mass × acceleration)
- E = ½mv² (Kinetic energy)
- PE = mgh (Gravitational potential energy)
- W = Fd (Work = force × distance)
- P = W/t (Power = work/time)

**Electricity:**
- V = IR (Voltage = current × resistance)
- P = VI (Power = voltage × current)
- E = Pt (Energy = power × time)

**Thermodynamics:**
- Q = mcΔT (Heat = mass × specific heat × temperature change)
- PV = nRT (Ideal gas law)
- η = W/Qin (Efficiency = work out / heat in)

**Waves:**
- v = fλ (Speed = frequency × wavelength)
- f = 1/T (Frequency = 1/period)

**Fluids:**
- P = F/A (Pressure = force/area)
- P = ρgh (Pressure = density × gravity × height)
